An interactive map of the routes and passenger volumes by route in Australia

The air travel route map indicates that the Melbourne to Sydney route is Australia's busiest, with over 221 million trips since 1984. The concentration of heavy passenger routes in the southeast, around cities like Brisbane, Sydney and Melbourne, highlights the demand for travel between densely populated regions. Factors contributing to this include the strong economic ties, frequent business travel, tourism, and limited alternative travel options, making flying the most efficient form of transport between these areas.

The color gradient illustrates that routes with lighter traffic tend to connect remote regions or less densely populated areas, such as the Northern Territory and parts of Western Australia, where air travel is less frequent due to lower population density and less economic activity. In these areas, flights may serve the mining sector, remote communities, or regional tourism.

The bottom graph, which can be applied as a date filter, shows a rise in the number of air routes between 1984 and 2024, which can be attributed to factors such as market liberalisation, the entrance of low-cost carriers, and an increase in consumer demand. The rise in routes also reflects airport expansions, improved infrastructure, and growing urban population, prompting airlines to increase their networks.

The slight plateau in unique route numbers in the mid 2010s suggests a temporary saturation or stabilisation of domestic routes, possibly due to consolidation in the airline industry or limited new demand in established markets. However, the uptick toward 2018-2024 could indicate renewed growth, possibly driven by regional development, new technologies, and increased tourism.
